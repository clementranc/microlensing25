Analysis of Gravitational Microlensing Event: OGLE-2018-BLG-0380

Sarang Shah  ✧  India Thirty Meter Telescope (TMT), India

We present the analysis of the microlensing event OGLE-2018-BLG-0380, that was observed towards the galactic bulge in the microlensing season of 2018. The light curve of this event had two prominent peaks and a third bump towards the decreasing side of the light curve. The conventional method of grid search failed to give any solutions that can explain the light curve. So, we performed a heuristic analysis of the light curve and found that the binary lens orbital motion explains the third perturbation. This is the highest orbital motion ever detected in a microlensing event. Since the event has \(t_\mathsf{E}\sim9\) days, we did not detect orbital parallax. We also investigated the occurrence of the event by binary lens and binary source. However, the binary lens and single-lens model is preferred by a significant probability. The Bayesian analysis of the galactic model priors that is constrained by the measured angular motion prefers the bulge location of the lens system by 91%. We find that the lens is comprised of an brown dwarf at the deuterium burning limit orbiting a M-dwarf mass host in the galactic bulge. The median projected separation between them is 0.85 AU. It is also found that the median value of (K.E./P.E.)\(_\perp\)= 0.91 which indicates a face-on system.
